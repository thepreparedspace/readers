which "oo" sound

"oo" makes two unrelated sounds in English, and the spelling gives no clue which one applies — the child has to simply know the word. Choose which sound family the child works with right now. In both mode, the long-oo sound is shown underlined everywhere it appears (the tab, the name-slip, and every word), so it's visually distinct from the short-oo sound without needing any extra words on screen.

how to present this work

Tap the small name-slip at the top of each section first — the child hears the one sound alone. Each word card below shows the whole word immediately, "oo" in red (underlined for the long sound). Tapping any single letter or the red "oo" plays a real recorded human voice — not synthesized speech — for that sound. Tapping the word itself reveals a speaker icon — tapping that speaks the whole word naturally, as a control of error the child can use on their own.

keeping it phonetic

Every word here is built only from sounds the child already has, apart from the target "oo" — there are no puzzle words in this set.

extending to the home

This reader is meant as an extension of the prepared environment into the home — usable by a parent unfamiliar with Montessori method, so the child can keep practicing independently between sessions.
